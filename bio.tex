\biography{%
Sean-Jiun Wang was born in West Palm Beach, Florida. After graduating from Suncoast High School with an interest in physics, he attended the University of Florida where he obtained a Bachelor of Science in physics and mathematics. For his graduate education, he remained at the University of Florida to pursue experimental high energy physics. Upon completing his coursework, he moved to the Franco-Swiss border where he began his graduate research with the CMS experiment at CERN. A year later, he was awarded the 2016 LPC Graduate Scholar fellowship and moved to Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory in Illinois where he would work closely with his advisor Dr. Jacobo Konigsberg until the end of his graduate research. He received a Doctor of Philosophy in the winter of 2018.
}
